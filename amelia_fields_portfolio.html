About

A polished portfolio for a musician whose work spans stage performance, education, and ocean-inspired creative direction.

Amelia Fields performs with a warm, confident style shaped by years of work in orchestral settings, pops ensembles, touring productions, opera, and regional theatre. Her career includes principal and assistant principal roles, featured solo work, and a long record of versatile collaboration across classical and commercial spaces.

Beyond performance, Amelia brings an unusual and compelling second lens to her work: she holds a degree in biology and is currently pursuing a master’s degree in oceanography, with a strong interest in ocean conservation. That combination gives her portfolio a point of view that feels thoughtful, contemporary, and memorable.

Music & Ocean Conservation

A meaningful second thread that sets this portfolio apart and creates room for interdisciplinary collaborations.

Amelia’s interest in ocean conservation is not a side note. It is part of the way she thinks about listening, resonance, and communication. With academic training in biology and graduate work in oceanography, she is especially interested in the relationship between sound and the marine world, including how music can help audiences connect more deeply with environmental change and marine life.

This perspective opens the door to artist talks, educational events, outreach partnerships, conservation-centered performances, and creative projects inspired by the ocean. It is also what makes this portfolio feel especially current: rooted in classical craft, but expansive enough to speak to larger questions about the world around us.
