About

A musician whose portfolio brings together classical training, live performance, and an ocean-minded creative perspective.

Amelia Fields performs with a warm, confident style shaped by years of work in orchestral settings, pops ensembles, touring productions, opera, chamber music, and regional theatre. Her experience includes principal and assistant principal roles, featured solo work, and a long record of versatile collaboration across classical and theatrical spaces.

Her artistic perspective is also informed by science. With a biology degree and ongoing graduate work in oceanography, Amelia is interested in how sound can help people connect with the natural world. That connection between music and marine science gives her work a point of view that is both personal and timely.

Music & Science

An interdisciplinary direction connecting performance, listening, marine life, and environmental awareness.

Amelia’s interest in ocean conservation is part of how she thinks about listening, resonance, and communication. Her academic background in biology and oceanography gives her a framework for exploring sound not only as music, but also as a way of paying attention to the living world.

This interdisciplinary focus supports future work in artist talks, educational outreach, conservation-centered performances, and creative projects inspired by marine soundscapes. It also reflects the central idea behind her developing practice: music can make scientific and environmental questions feel more immediate, emotional, and human.
